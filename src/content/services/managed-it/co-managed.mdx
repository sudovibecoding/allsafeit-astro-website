## **When co-managed IT services make sense**

Co-managed IT isn't for everyone. It works best in specific scenarios.

You have an internal IT team that's capable but overwhelmed. They're handling routine support tickets when they should be working on strategic initiatives. Co-managed services handle the routine work, freeing your team for higher-value projects.

Your business is growing faster than your IT team can scale. Adding headcount takes time. Training takes longer. Co-managed IT gives you immediate access to specialized expertise without the months-long hiring process.

You're tackling a major IT project that requires specialized skills. Maybe it's migrating to cloud infrastructure, implementing new security measures, or managing compliance requirements. Your internal team has general IT knowledge, but this project needs specialists. Co-managed services fill those skill gaps.

You need 24/7 coverage but can't afford a full night shift. Your internal team deserves time off. Co-managed IT provides round-the-clock monitoring and support without burning out your staff.

Your business needs fluctuate seasonally or project-based. Co-managed IT services scale with your business needs, providing additional support during peak times without carrying excess capacity year-round.

## **Co-managed vs fully managed IT services**

The difference between co-managed and fully managed IT comes down to control and responsibility.

With fully managed IT services, an external provider takes full responsibility for your entire IT environment. They become your IT department. This works well for businesses without internal IT staff or those wanting to focus entirely on their core business.

With co-managed IT, your internal team stays in charge. They maintain control over IT strategy, priorities, and day-to-day operations. The external provider supports specific functions, fills skill gaps, or provides specialized expertise.

Co-managed services let you keep the institutional knowledge your internal IT staff has built while accessing enterprise-grade tools and expertise. You decide what to keep in-house and what to outsource based on your business needs and team capabilities.

[Fully managed services](/services/managed-it) work when you want technology handled completely by experts. Co-managed services work when you want to enhance the team you already have.

## The Report Card, Not the Trophy

When [CRN](https://www.crn.com/) releases the [MSP 500](https://www.crn.com/rankings-and-lists/msp2026-details?c=165) each year, it's not just a popularity contest. It's a snapshot of the managed service providers in North America that are driving growth, delivering value, and helping businesses get more out of their technology investments without breaking the budget.

Getting recognized once is validating. Getting recognized seven times (2017, 2018, 2021, 2022, 2024, 2025, and now 2026) means we're doing something consistently right, and adapting when the landscape shifts.

Because it has shifted. A lot.

### What's Changed in Seven Years

When we first made the MSP 500 list in 2017, the managed services landscape looked different:

**Then:** Most businesses outsourced IT because they didn't have internal staff.  
**Now:** Many businesses _have_ internal IT, but those teams are stretched thin, fighting fires, and don't have depth in specialized areas like cybersecurity, cloud architecture, or AI implementation.

**Then**: Cybersecurity was a line item. A firewall and antivirus software. Check the box.  
**Now:** It's existential. Ransomware, business email compromise, supply chain attacks: these aren't hypotheticals. They're Tuesday afternoons.

**Then**: "AI" meant chatbots and science fiction.  
**Now**: It's workflow automation, document processing, and business intelligence that produce measurable productivity gains. Not experimental. Deployed. Working.

We've evolved with those shifts. And that evolution, not the recognition itself, is what keeps us on this list.

We're not here to replace your IT team. We're here to amplify them.

Many of our clients have a strong internal IT person or small team. They handle the day-to-day. But when it comes to designing a zero-trust security architecture, migrating a hybrid cloud environment, or implementing AI-driven automation, they need specialized expertise.

That's where co-managed IT comes in. We partner with your existing team. We bring depth in cybersecurity, cloud, and automation. Your people stay in the driver's seat. We're the force multiplier.

Which is the entire point.

### What to Look for in an MSP Partner in 2026

If you're evaluating managed service providers, whether you're replacing an existing partner or bringing one on for the first time, here's what to ask:

**1\. Do they understand co-managed IT?**  
If you have internal IT staff, you don't need someone to replace them. You need a partner who respects their expertise and fills the gaps. Ask: _"How do you work with existing IT teams?"_

**2\. Can they talk cybersecurity in plain English?**  
If a provider leads with acronyms and certifications without explaining what they actually _do_ for your business, run. Ask: _"How do you protect against ransomware, and what happens if we get hit anyway?"_

**3\. Are they implementing AI, or just talking about it?**  
Everyone's pitching AI now. Ask for specifics: _"What AI solutions have you deployed for businesses like ours, and what were the measurable results?"_

**4\. Do they have a track record?**  
Industry recognition (like the MSP 500) is one signal. Longevity is another. Client retention is the biggest. Ask: _"How long have your clients been with you, and why do they stay?"_

**5\. Do they sound like a human or a script?**  
If the sales conversation feels like vendor wallpaper ("comprehensive," "strategic partner," "cutting-edge solutions"), you're not getting a partner. You're getting a commodity. Trust your gut.

## **What is BBB Accreditation and why does it matter for IT support?**

BBB accreditation is a voluntary certification that a service provider meets the Better Business Bureau’s standards for trust, honesty, and responsiveness. By committing to these guidelines, an accredited business signals to enterprises that it operates transparently, resolves disputes fairly, and maintains high customer satisfaction.

### **What are the key BBB standards for IT service providers?**

The Better Business Bureau's Code of Business Practices outlines eight "Standards for Trust" that accredited businesses must uphold. These standards cover principles such as building trust, honest advertising, transparency, honoring promises, responsiveness, safeguarding privacy, and embodying integrity, which are designed to enhance customer confidence.

Below is an overview of core standards that directly influence service quality and reliability:

| Standard | Definition | Impact on IT Support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Transparency | Clear policies and accurate service descriptions | Ensures clients understand SLAs and costs |
| Responsiveness | Prompt replies to inquiries and complaints | Guarantees rapid help-desk turnaround |
| Integrity | Honest advertising and no hidden fees | Builds predictable budgets and trust |
| Privacy Protection | Safeguarding customer data and confidentiality | Strengthens cybersecurity posture |
| Dispute Resolution | Formal process for handling complaints | Provides recourse, reducing service gaps |

## **Must Know IT Support Terms:**

Even when you would be trusting an experienced and specialized IT service provider to take care of the support infrastructure of your company, you still should be aware of some IT terms and know what they mean. This information would not only help you understand the IT infrastructure and support established within your business, but would also help you communicate with the service provider with ease. Here are some common terms that you should be aware of:

-   Business Intelligence:

As already discussed, one of the most important roles of an IT service provider is to ensure that the data is being collected, analyzed and utilized in a productive manner. Business Intelligence refers to the set of procedures and the architecture that is concerned with data storage, data fetching and also the analysis of the data to derive insights that can be detrimental to the growth and prosperity of the business.

-   [BYOD](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kVNDl7ig1mk):

Some businesses, especially the small scale ones who are still in the phase of setting up and are short on budget to be able to invest on in-house devices, encourage their employees to bring their own devices and use them for work. This is also referred to as BYOD or “Bring Your Own Device”. This may seem convenient but in reality, when opting for BYOD, the company has to take some measures that ensure data privacy and be a hundred percent sure that the employees do not end up misusing or abusing any sensitive information regarding the company.

-   Cloud Computing:

You might have already heard this term a couple of times. Due to the extensive range of data that we are dealing with these days, solely relying on physical data storage mediums is not enough. Therefore, the modern age is the age of cloud computing. [**IT support for small businesses**](https://www.allsafeit.com/services/it-consulting) provides cloud computing services that allows businesses to store their data on cloud based storages and also use cloud based servers and equipment to run their technological services.

-   Data Center:

The meaning of the term data center is quite evident from the name itself. It is a space that is dedicated to the storage and handling of data. It can be a physical space as well as a dedicated slot on cloud where all the storage facilities and services are based out of.

-   Disaster Recovery:

Things are unpredictable and no matter how safe and secure a business and its IT infrastructure is, there is still a chance for something to go wrong. This is where the **IT services company** comes in and helps with disaster recovery. The goal of disaster recovery is to be able to bounce back and reinstate the IT devices and infrastructure as soon as they can.
